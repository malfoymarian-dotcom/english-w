🌍 Regional Context: France in Europe
Key EU member
Strategic location
Franco-German engine

France borders Belgium, Germany, Switzerland, Italy, Spain, and Monaco. It shares Latin roots with Italy and Spain but is more secular and linear-active. Unlike Colombia (Andean region, Pacific Alliance), France is a leader in NATO and the G7. For Colombians: European alliances and bureaucracy are more rigid than Latin American dynamics.

📜 History, Religion & Current Behavior
French Revolution (1789)
Notre Dame: religious legacy
Laïcité: secular education

Key historical events: French Revolution (liberty, equality, fraternity), Napoleonic Code, World Wars, May 1968 protests. Laïcité (constitutional secularism) means the state is neutral regarding religion. Only ~8% of French people practice regularly, though ~50% identify as Catholic. Islam is the second religion (8-10%).

How history & religion shape behavior today: The French value critical debate, avoid public religious displays, and separate private faith from professional life. Contrast with Colombia: Colombian society is predominantly Catholic (70%+), religious symbols are common in workplaces, and church influences social events. A Colombian should never ask “What church do you attend?” in French business settings.

⏳ Edward Hall’s concepts (≥2)

Monochronic: punctuality is key
Colombia: high-context warmth

Monochronic vs polychronic time: France is monochronic — schedules, deadlines, and one task at a time. Colombians are more polychronic. Arriving 5 minutes late for a business meeting in Paris is disrespectful.
High/low context: France is moderately high-context (uses irony, sophisticated references). Colombia is also high-context but relies more on non-verbal warmth. Colombians may interpret French directness as cold; French may see Colombian effusiveness as unprofessional.

📘 Richard Lewis Model (≥2 categories)

France: task-oriented, linear-active
Colombia: multi-active, relationship-first

Linear-active vs multi-active: French are logical, data-driven, and separate personal from professional. Colombians build trust before business. Direct vs indirect communication: French say “non” clearly; Colombians may say “maybe” to preserve harmony. Be explicit.

👁️ Nonverbal Communication (4+ types)

Firm handshake (don’t overdo)
Proxemics: keep ~60 cm distance
La bise: only among friends
⚠️ The OK sign (circle) means zero / insult
  • Proxemics: Maintain arm’s length. Colombians standing too close may make French uncomfortable.
  • Chronemics: Arrive exactly on time. No “Colombian hour”.
  • Kinesics: Avoid pointing with index finger. Never use the “OK” circle (offensive).
  • Vocalics: Moderate tone; avoid loud conversations in restaurants.
  • Artifacts: Dress elegantly (suit/blazer) for business.
  • Haptics: Handshake for first meetings; la bise only with close acquaintances.

✅ Do's & ❌ Don'ts for Colombians in France

DO's
• Always greet with “Bonjour” before anything.
• Use Monsieur/Madame + last name until invited to use first name.
• Be punctual for business and formal appointments.
• Prepare logical arguments; the French appreciate intellectual debate.
• Say “merci” and maintain composure.
DON'Ts
• Don’t ask about religion, salary, or politics too early.
• Don’t make the “OK” gesture (thumb-index circle).
• Don’t interrupt constantly; let the French finish their point.
• Don’t expect them to speak Spanish; learn basic French phrases.
• Don’t give effusive hugs or backslaps in professional settings.
